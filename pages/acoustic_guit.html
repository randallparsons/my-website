The Acoustic Guitar

Before we get started, let's take a look under the hood...


Fingering:
The placement of the fingers may seem alien at first. It is probably easiest to get used to if you just look at the basic principles involved. You ideally want clean direct contact between your finger tip and the fret board on top of the string. It is important to know that you are not putting your finger on top of the fret itself! You are ideally placing your fingertip a depressing the string against the fret board just behind the fret. In the chord charts it looks like the fingertip is placed midway between the frets, but that is not actually going to work well at all. Another important point is that your leverage against the string will be best if you can achieve a contact point that is nearly perpendicular to the fret board.

Holding a Pick:
You do not need to do the pinch of death when holding a pick. You want just enough pressure to keep it in your grasp, but not so tight that you can't glide across the strings. This of the pick as a surfboard and the strings as the waves. You just want a clean smooth glide. You are not trying to saw the strings in half!

Hand Placement:
The ideal hand placement is with the outer heal of your hand resting lightly against the outside of the bridge of the guitar near the soundhole and pick-guard. The outside of the wrist can be kept bouncing against the bridge as you strum, just try not to interfere too much with the strings (unless of course you are working on more percussive strumming!)
